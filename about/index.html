Governments like to make promises and committing to programs that make them look good. The Open Government Partnership is one of the programs where it is easy to get the good lable without actually delivering anything along the ideas of it. It is important to hold government accountable for what it promises. Publicly tracking promises, deliverance and responses is one way to do this. 'Where is the Data?' tracks requests for open data to give the government a sense of what is wanted and to show where the government fails to deliver data

'Where is the Data?' was conceived and created by Michael Bauer. It is built on github/jekyll/bootstrap/recline and google docs.
